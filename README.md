# Chicago Food Inspections
Work in class with dataset from https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/chicago/chicago-food-inspections?resource=download&select=food-inspections.csv

# Questions

Q1) Which types of food establishments are inspected most frequently?

Q2) Do high-risk establishments fail more often than low-risk ones?

Q3) How are inspection outcomes distributed across different risk categories?

Q4) Has the failure rate changed over time?

Q5) Are there seasonal patterns in inspections or failures?

Q6) Are inspections more likely to result in failure during certain periods?

Q7) Which ZIP codes or areas record the highest number of failed inspections?

Q8) Which areas have the highest failure rate, normalized by number of inspections?

Q9) Are some establishments inspected much more frequently than others?

Q10) How often do establishments pass an inspection after previously failing one?

Q11) Which establishments show the greatest improvement over time?

Q12) Which establishments show repeated non-compliance over time?

Q13) Which types of violations are most common in failed inspections?

Q14) Which violation patterns appear most frequently in failed inspections compared to passed ones?

Q15) Are some violations strongly associated with specific types of establishments?

Q16) Is the number of violations recorded related to the inspection outcome?

Q17) Do high-risk establishments receive more violations on average?

Q18) Are follow-up inspections more likely to be passed compared to initially failed inspections?

Q19) How long does it take on average for an establishment to “recover” after a failed inspection?

Q20) Are repeated failures concentrated in a small subset of establishments?
